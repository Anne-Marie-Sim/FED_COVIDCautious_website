Coronavirus (COVID-19)
Vaccination

Why is it important to get vaccinated?

COVID-19 vaccination protects you against severe disease, minimises the risk of transmission, and protects our healthcare system from being overwhelmed. Vaccination is free under the National Vaccination Programme for all.

What are the available vaccinations in Singapore?

1. Pfizer-BioNTech/Comirnaty
2. Moderna/Spikevax 3. Sinovac-CoronaVac

Pfizer-BioNTech and Moderna have attained full registration successfully. The inclusion of the Sinovac-CoronaVac vaccine in the National

Vaccination Programme (NVP)* will persist until September 30, 2024. If there are no applications for full registration of the Sinovac-CoronaVac vaccine by that date, its inclusion in the NVP will cease. However, the Sinovac-CoronaVac vaccine will remain accessible at private clinics approved for its usage through the Special Access Route (SAR).

The Novavax/Nuvaxovid vaccine is presently not available under the NVP due to the discontinuation of the original Novavax/Nuvaxovid vaccine by the manufacturer. Novavax is currently in the process of seeking regulatory approval for its updated COVID-19 XBB.1.5 vaccine formulation.

*Within the National Vaccination Programme, vaccination is provided free of charge to all Singaporean Citizens, Permanent Residents, Long Term Pass Holders, and certain Short Term Pass holders.

What should I do to be vaccinated against COVID-19?

Persons who are 6 months to 4 years should complete the primary series of vaccination with:

1. Two doses of Moderna/Spikevax, given eight weeks apart, OR
2. Three doses of Pfizer-BioNTech/Comirnaty, given eight weeks apart

Persons who are aged 5 years and above should adhere to the following vaccination recommendations:

For minimum protection:

1. Three doses of Pfizer-BioNTech/Comirnaty, Moderna/Spikevax or Novavax/Nuvaxovid. The first and second doses should be spaced eight weeks apart, while the recommended interval between the second and third doses is five months
2. Four doses of Sinovac-CoronaVac. The first and second doses should be spaced eight weeks apart. Thereafter, the recommended interval between the second and third, as well as third and fourth doses is three months. These would be doses that were previously referred to as the primary vaccination series and the first additional dose.

For additional protection:

An additional dose of the updated vaccine at 1 year after the last dose is recommended in the following persons. They can start to receive it from five months after their last dose.

1. All persons aged 60 years and above;
2. Medically vulnerable persons; and
Residents of aged care facilities

Encouragement is given for individuals aged 6 months and older to receive an additional dose of the updated vaccine, especially targeting healthcare workers, along with household members and caregivers of medically vulnerable individuals. The Pfizer-BioNTech/Comirnaty and Moderna/Spikevax vaccines are suggested for this purpose. Individuals medically ineligible for mRNA vaccines may opt for Novavax/Nuvaxovid* or Sinovac-CoronaVac as alternatives.

Where can I get vaccinated in Singapore?

Individuals eligible for COVID-19 vaccination have the option to visit any Joint Testing and Vaccination Centre (JTVC) or polyclinic without prior appointment. Alternatively, appointments can be made at a JTVC or Public Health Preparedness Clinic (PHPC) via the provided link.
